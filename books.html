READING LIST

The Age of AI

by Henry Kissinger, Eric Schmidt

The book explores the ethical implications of AI and how it can be used to benefit humanity, as well as the potential dangers it poses. Through interviews with leading experts and analysis of current events, the authors offer a comprehensive look at the role of AI in our rapidly changing world.

Read More

The AI Delusion

by Gary Smith

AI has the potential to revolutionize certain industries and improve our lives in various ways. He examines the limitations of current AI technologies, the ethical implications of relying on AI, and the potential risks of an over-reliance on AI. Through this book, Smith aims to provide a balanced perspective on AI and help readers make informed decisions about its role in our lives.

Superintelligence

by Nick Bostrom

superintelligent AI surpassing human control and the importance of developing and implementing proper safeguards to prevent such a scenario from occurring. The book also examines the potential benefits of superintelligence, such as solving complex global problems and revolutionizing industries, and the ethical considerations involved in creating and utilizing such technology.

The Singularity is Near

by Ray Kurzweil

Kurzweil predicts that this moment will happen in the near future and explores the potential implications for society and the individual. He argues that the singularity will bring about exponential technological advancements and ultimately lead to a utopian future. However, he also acknowledges the potential dangers of such a future and the need for careful planning and ethical considerations.

Our Final Invention

by James Barrat

Barrat delves into the ethical concerns surrounding the creation of superintelligent machines and the potential consequences of relinquishing control to them. Through interviews with leading AI researchers and experts in the field, Barrat raises alarm about the potential for AI to surpass human intelligence and potentially pose a threat to humanity.
